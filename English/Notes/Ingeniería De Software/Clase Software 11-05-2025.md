# Ethics and Regulations in Software Reliability

## Introduction

Reliability and ethics in software development are pillars to guarantee the proper functioning, security and social acceptance of technological products. In addition, the regulatory framework establishes legal obligations that protect users and companies.

---

## Importance of Reliability

| Appearance | Description |
|-------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Risk reduction | Unreliable systems can lead to data loss and financial risks. |
| Competitiveness | Companies with reliable software gain preference and reputation in the market.  |
| Regulatory compliance | Reliable software helps comply with data security and privacy laws. |

---

## Professional Ethics

| Principle | Application in Software Engineering |
|-------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Protect users | Security and privacy in data management.   |
| Act with integrity | Transparency and honesty in development. |
| Avoid misuse of software | Prevent malicious functions and fraud.     |
| Social responsibility | Positive impact on users and society.     |

---

## Key Ethical Actions

- **Ensure quality**: Extensive testing and proper documentation.
- **Security**: Implement measures against unauthorized access.
- **Prevention**: Audit, control access and foresee risks.
- **Standards compliance**: Follow IEEE, ACM codes and legal regulations.

---

## Computer Crimes

| Crime | Description |
|-------------------|-----------------------------|
| Phishing | Identity theft for fraud.      |
| Piracy | Illegal access and unauthorized distribution. |
| Identity theft | Unauthorized use of personal data.      |
| Ransomware | Data kidnapping through extortion.      |
